Raspberry Brownies

Description

Ah, the humble raspberry - god amongst fruit, jewel amongst treasures. Fruit is generally difficult for me to eat, but raspberries are perfect. They also pair incredibly well with chocolate when you are in need of a tasty treat.

I suppose you could make this with other small fruits if you want. I've done it once with cherries (which I don't even like - long story) and I don't see why it wouldn't work with something else. But if you want my advice - and, implicitly, you do, since you're reading my website - don't fix what isn't broken.

Ingredients

  • 0.75 cups plain flour
  • 1.5 cups soft brown sugar (packed)
  • 0.5 cups cocoa powder
  • 250g salted Lurpak spread
  • 4 eggs
  • 200g dark chocolate
  • 110g milk chocolate
  • Punnet of raspberries

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 160C.
  2. Cover a baking tray with baking paper.
  3. Put the chocolate, butter and sugar in a pan over a gentle heat to melt.
  4. Stir in the eggs.
  5. Sieve in the flour and cocoa.
  6. Mix in the raspberries.
  7. Spread the mixture across the baking tray.
  8. Bake for 30 minutes.
  9. Cut into squares (or whatever else you want) and leave to cool.
  10. Enjoy a delicious brownie after all that hard work!
